The Brief

The assignment, clearly stated.

Our Process

A motion system built for keynote timelines.

We don't run a full production cycle on a 29-day clock. We run a precision execution track. Every step is designed to compress decision-making, eliminate revision risk, and deliver files that work flawlessly on event day.

  • 01 Script & Asset Intake
  • 02 Motion Style Frames
  • 03 Animation & Build
  • 04 Review Cycles
  • 05 Speaker Integration

01

Step 01

Script & Asset Intake

We ingest AWS-provided script, storyboard, and demo outline on day one. Our motion director reviews for pacing, visual opportunity, and technical specs before a single frame is touched. Misalignments surface here, not in round two.

02

Step 02

Motion Style Frames

Before full build, we develop 2-3 style frames showing exactly how the demo visuals will look and move: UI treatment, callout style, pacing, and transition language. One focused alignment moment that eliminates downstream revision risk. This is where we blend real product UI with abstracted visuals to focus on what truly matters.

03

Step 03

Animation & Build

Approved style frames go straight into full production. Senior motion designers build each demo sequence to keynote spec: UI elements, animated callouts, zoom and focus work, transitions, and pacing timed to the speaker script. We work in the toolset that serves the output, optimized for quality, not habit.

04

Step 04

Review Cycles

Structured async review via the SketchDeck platform. Annotate directly on video frames, track revisions in context, and resolve feedback without email chains. Typically two focused rounds, enough to sharpen without burning the timeline. You always know exactly where the work stands.

05

Step 05

Speaker Integration

Final assets delivered in both formats: a standalone demo video file, and a PowerPoint with the demo sequence broken across individual slides so the speaker drives the pacing live on stage. Both versions reviewed for timing, sync, and speaker usability, not just visual quality.

06

Step 06

Event-Day Readiness

Full technical QA pass: file formats, aspect ratios, resolution, playback testing, and backup exports. You get assets that work on any AV setup without a last-minute scramble backstage. We send a technical brief alongside every final delivery so your AV team has zero ambiguity.
