## SECTION 3: OUR PROCESS & APPROACH

**Section heading:** How We Work

**Subheading:** A production system built for executive speed and C-suite quality.

This section should be presented as a visual step-by-step process flow — ideally horizontal on desktop, vertical on mobile. Each step gets an icon, a short title, and a description.

### Step 1: Intake & Alignment
**Icon concept:** Target / compass

Every engagement starts with a structured creative brief — audience, singular objective, and what success looks like in the room. For recurring work like this, the brief gets faster over time as we internalize Julia's voice, her storytelling preferences, and how AWS frames big ideas. By deck three, we're aligned before the kickoff call ends.

### Step 2: Narrative Architecture
**Icon concept:** Blueprint / outline

Slides are the last thing we think about. First, we map the story: what's the throughline, where does the argument peak, what data has to land emotionally — not just factually. We work with your content team to turn raw talking points into a designed experience with a beginning, middle, and a close that sticks.

### Step 3: Visual Design & Build
**Icon concept:** Pen tool / paintbrush

Our senior designers translate narrative into high-fidelity slides — bold where it needs to be, precise where it matters, always on-brand. We work natively in PowerPoint, Google Slides, and Keynote, so every file is editable and production-ready the moment you receive it. No Figma handoff lag. No translation artifacts.

### Step 4: Rapid Revision Cycles
**Icon concept:** Lightning bolt / clock

Executive decks change fast. Content shifts. Speakers evolve their message at 11pm the night before. We staff this account for that reality — same-day and next-day revision cycles, clear version control, and a single thread per project so nothing gets lost. The platform always has the most up-to-date design. You'll never be hunting for the latest file.

### Step 5: Final Delivery & Speaker-Ready QA
**Icon concept:** Checkmark / stage

Before anything leaves our hands, every deck goes through a full QA pass: animation timing, font embedding, aspect ratio, backup PDF export, speaker notes formatting. You get a file that works in any AV setup, on any screen, without a last-minute scramble backstage.

### Step 6: Post-Event Debrief & Optimization
**Icon concept:** Loop / refresh arrows

After each engagement, we debrief on what landed — what got audience reactions, what the speaker loved, what we'd sharpen. That feedback becomes the baseline for the next deck. Every build for AWS gets tighter than the last because we're not starting from scratch — we're compounding.

**Closing line for this section:**
We've run this system for Salesforce keynotes, Okta main stages, and Microsoft executive comms. It works. We adapt it to your cadence, your speakers, and your stakes.

## BRAND / TONE NOTES FOR DEVELOPER

- **Voice:** Confident, direct, peer-to-peer. We're not pitching up to AWS — we're showing them a partner who operates at their level. No filler words, no corporate fluff, no "we'd love the opportunity to..." energy.
- **Visual tone:** Dark, premium, cinematic. Think keynote stage lighting: deep blacks, sharp whites, orange and amber accents. AWS's own palette (navy #232F3E, orange #FF9900) can inform accent colors but should not dominate — this is our brand showing up strong.
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif. Large headlines. Generous whitespace.
- **Imagery:** Use abstract stage/light photography or subtle 3D renders. No stock photography of people in meeting rooms. If showing portfolio work, use actual slide screenshots at high resolution.
- **Interaction:** Smooth scroll, subtle parallax, hover states that feel responsive and alive.
- **Platform section:** Embed video prominently. Feature list should echo the visual style of the attached platform slide (dark navy background, yellow/lime left-border callouts, bold feature labels).
