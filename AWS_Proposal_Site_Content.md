## SECTION 3: OUR PROCESS & APPROACH

**Section heading:** How We Work

**Subheading:** A production system built for executive speed and C-suite quality.

This section should be presented as a visual step-by-step process flow — ideally horizontal on desktop, vertical on mobile. Each step gets an icon, a short title, and a description.

### Step 1: Intake & Alignment
**Icon concept:** Target / compass

We start every deck with a structured creative brief: who's the audience, what's the one thing they need to walk away with, and what does success look like? For recurring engagements like this, the brief becomes faster over time as we learn Julia's voice, preferences, and storytelling rhythm.

### Step 2: Narrative Architecture
**Icon concept:** Blueprint / outline

Before we touch a single slide, we map the story arc. What's the throughline? Where are the emotional peaks? What data needs to land? We work with your content team to shape raw talking points into a compelling narrative structure — not just a sequence of slides but a designed experience.

### Step 3: Visual Design & Build
**Icon concept:** Pen tool / paintbrush

Our senior designers translate the narrative into high-fidelity slides — balancing bold visual impact with clarity and brand consistency. We work natively in PowerPoint and Keynote, so files are always editable and production-ready. No Figma-to-deck translation lag.

### Step 4: Rapid Revision Cycles
**Icon concept:** Lightning bolt / clock

This is where the rubber meets the road for executive decks. Content shifts. Messaging evolves. Speakers change their minds at 11pm. We staff this account with designers who are available for same-day and next-day revision cycles — with clear version control and a single Slack/email thread per project.

### Step 5: Final Delivery & Speaker-Ready QA
**Icon concept:** Checkmark / stage

Every deck goes through a final QA pass: animation timing, font embedding, aspect ratio checks, backup PDF export, and speaker notes formatting. We deliver files that are ready to plug into any AV setup without surprises.

### Step 6: Post-Event Debrief & Optimization
**Icon concept:** Loop / refresh arrows

After each engagement, we capture what worked — what got audience reactions, what the speaker loved, what we'd evolve. This feedback loop means every deck we build for AWS gets sharper than the last.

**Closing line for this section:**
We've run this playbook for Salesforce keynotes, Okta main stages, and Microsoft executive comms. The system works. We adapt it to your cadence.

## BRAND / TONE NOTES FOR DEVELOPER

- **Voice:** Confident, direct, peer-to-peer. We're not pitching up to AWS — we're showing them a partner who operates at their level. No filler words, no corporate fluff, no "we'd love the opportunity to..." energy.
- **Visual tone:** Dark, premium, cinematic. Think keynote stage lighting: deep blacks, sharp whites, orange and amber accents. AWS's own palette (navy #232F3E, orange #FF9900) can inform accent colors but should not dominate — this is our brand showing up strong.
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif. Large headlines. Generous whitespace.
- **Imagery:** Use abstract stage/light photography or subtle 3D renders. No stock photography of people in meeting rooms. If showing portfolio work, use actual slide screenshots at high resolution.
- **Interaction:** Smooth scroll, subtle parallax, hover states that feel responsive and alive. The pricing calculator should feel like a product experience, not a form.
